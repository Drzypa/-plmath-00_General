\subsection{Introduction}
 \emph{Biogroupoids}, $\mathcal{G_B}$, were introduced as mathematical representations of 
evolving biological species (\cite{BBGG2k6, BBG2k7}) generated by \emph{weakly equivalent classes of living organisms}, $E_O$, specified by inter-breeding organisms;in this case, a \emph{weak equivalence relation}, $\sim_w$, is defined on the set of evolving organisms modeled in terms of functional, \emph{isomorphic genome networks}, $G_{iso}^N$, such as those described by $LM_n$-logic networks in \L{}ukasiewicz-Moisil, $\LM$ topoi (\cite{BBGG2k6}).  
 
\subsection{AT-Formulation}
  
 This concept allows an algebraic topology formulation of the origin of species and biological evolution
both at organismal/organismic and biomolecular levels; it represents a new approach to biological evolution 
from the standpoint of super-complex systems biology.
