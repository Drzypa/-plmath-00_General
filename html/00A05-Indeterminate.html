indeterminate


An indeterminate  is simply a variable that is not known or solvable.  It is usually denoted by a mathematical alphabet (x, y, z, or α, β, etc…).  It is important to distinguish between a variable and an indeterminate in that a variable is solvable, at least conditionally.  To make this more precise, let’s see two examples:

  1. 1.

    Let x be a variable such that  2+3⁢x=a+b⁢x, where a,b∈ℚ. Then  x=(a-2)/(3-b).  Here x is solvable conditioned on the equation given. Any values of a and b(≠3) will yield a value for x.

  2. 2.

    Let x be an indeterminate such that  2+3⁢x=a+b⁢x, where  a,b∈ℚ.  Since x can not be solved, we have  2=a  and  3=b.  Note that if a and b are previously assigned to be values other than 2 and 3 respectively, then x is no longer an indeterminate.
